Research Summary - Mantis Shrimp

Below research summary was divided into three sections. Firstly, it would provide some background information about mantis shrimps. Next, it explained some of the reasons that make mantis shrimps sucessful predators. The summary ended with some fun facts about this animal.

Intro

Scientific classification

Mantis shrimp is categorized as a subclass of crustaceans, which includes many aquatic animals that have thick exoskeleton and multiple pairs of legs, for example lobster, crab, shrimp, etc. Reportedly, typical size of a fully grown mantis shrimp is approximately 10 cm; largest mantis shrimp ever record was measured at 46 cm in length.

Location and habitat

Mantis shrimps usually live in tropical or sub-tropical waters. Some species prefer hiding in rocks while others prefer burrowing in the seabed. All mantis shrimps are aggressive carnivorous; sometimes they hunt and chase for a kill.

Physical characteristics

Among other crustaceans, mantis shrimps can be recognized by their iconic appearance. They look like lobsters but instead of two big clamps, mantis shrimps have two front legs that resemble mantis’ front legs. Mantis shrimps can be categorized in two group, spearing or smashing, depend on the shape of their front legs.

Hunting methods

Spearer mantis shrimps usually buries themselves beneath the ocean bed and use their spear-like front legs to impale the preys that pass by. On the other hand, smasher mantis shrimps hide in the coral and use their club-like front legs to knock down their preys with brute force.

Research-Findings

World strongest animal?

There are several attempts ( for example here and here ) to shortlist the strongest animals in the world, some rankings even measure how much weight these animals can move in comparison with their body weight. In this way, strength is assessed regardless of their size. For example, dung beetle is the strongest animal because it can pull the weight that is more than one thousand times heavier than itself. However, mantis shrimps are not featured in any of those lists because they don't excel in weight lifting. On the other hand, Guinness World Records and BBC listed mantis shrimp as the animal with the most powerful punch in the animal kingdom.

A research about predator and prey concluded that in order to be a sucessful predator that excels within their hunting environment, one needs to have a good combination of senses and physical adaptations. It seems that mantis shirmps' speed, brute force of their strike, impresive vision, and shell structure tick all the boxes and turn them into formidable hunters.

Abilities' break down

How hard can they hit?

Reportedly, peacock mantis shrimp, a popular species among mantis shrimp, can swing its clubs with the force that is equally strong as a bullet shot from a .22 caliber gun. Some sources claimed that the force from these swings could be as much as 150kg, or about 2,500 times the peacock mantis shrimp’s weight.

How fast can they hit?

Sheer power alone only tells half of the story of mantis shrimp’s punches. The only thing that is more impressive than the force of their punches is the speed that those punches were thrown. Reportedly, peacock mantis shrimp can accelerate their clubs at the speed of more than 80 km/h. Most importantly, one strike can happen in less than 800 microseconds. For comparison, on average, our eye blink is between 100-150 milliseconds . Therefore, theoretically, if a mantis shrimp could keep punching continuously, we would miss 500 shrimp’s punches if we blink once. While not as powerful as smasher type, spearer type mantis shrimps use this extremely speed to impale their prey.

Why don't their front legs break after those powerful strikes?

In order to protect their front legs from breaking under reaction force, these clubs have a special shock absorbent mechanism that is shaped at molecular level. Scientists named this structure bouligand. In short, this structure allows small cracks to prevent total collapse/ breakage.

How can it detect the preys?

As if super force and super speed is not enough, mantis shrimps also have super vision. Reportedly, their visual spectrum is broader than any other species in animal kingdom, thanks to the 16 colour receptors in their eyes; for comparison, human only has three. This allows manthis shrims to see polarized light (light that reflected from a flat surface, like mirror or fish scale, etc.) and give them a hughe advantage in their environment. On top of that, their eyes are on the stalks that move freely in various angles; hence they can have incredibly good view of their surroundings.

Fun Facts

  • The punches of smasher type mantis shrimps are so strong and fast that they also create bubbles near their clubs. These under-water bubbles collapse immediately and release a flash of light and the heat that is as hot as the sun’s surface. Therefore, these punches do not need to connect to make the kill.
  • Mantis shrimps can break glass tank. Hence, if kept as pet, they should be put in a hard plastic tank.
  • Mantis shrimp's bouligand structure is so special that scientists study it in hope of producing lighter and stronger materials for car frame as well as explosion shield.
